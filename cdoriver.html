Cagayan De Oro River

The Cagayan de Oro River, commonly called CDO River, is a major river in Northern Mindanao, Philippines. It flows through the city of Cagayan de Oro and nearby areas in Misamis Oriental and Bukidnon. Its source is in the Kalatungan and Kitanglad Mountain Ranges in Bukidnon, and it travels northward to empty into Macajalar Bay near the city’s coast. The river is approximately 90 kilometers long, with numerous tributaries feeding it as it winds through valleys and forests. The river basin supports local ecosystems and communities, providing water, fishery resources, and natural scenic beauty.

🌄 Tourism & Local Importance

Cagayan de Oro City is known as the Whitewater Rafting Capital of the Philippines largely because of this river. Beyond rafting, the river provides scenic views of lush hills, limestone cliffs, and verdant valleys. Efforts are underway by local groups and councils to protect and sustainably manage the river basin and its watershed.
