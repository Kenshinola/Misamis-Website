🌏 About

Misamis Oriental is a coastal province in the Northern Mindanao region of the Philippines. It blends bustling urban centers like Cagayan de Oro with lush forests, rolling mountains, and scenic beaches along the Bohol Sea — offering a unique mix of culture, nature, and adventure.

📍 Geography & Population

The province covers 3,131.52 square kilometers and has a population of around 956,900 (2020). It is composed of 23 municipalities and 2 highly urbanized cities, with Cagayan de Oro as the provincial capital. Its varied topography — from coastal plains to interior highlands — supports agriculture, trade, and tourism.

🏗️ Economy & Infrastructure

The province thrives on agro-industrial production, logistics, and trade through the port of Cagayan de Oro. Key industries include agribusiness, food processing, manufacturing, and tourism. With the Laguindingan Airport and major highways, Misamis Oriental is well-connected to the rest of Mindanao.

🌿 Attractions & Tourism

Nature lovers can explore Sagpulon Falls in Jasaan or the Initao-Libertad Protected Landscape. Thrill-seekers can enjoy whitewater rafting along the Cagayan de Oro River. Cultural and spiritual landmarks — like the Divine Mercy Shrine in El Salvador — showcase the rich history and traditions of the province.

🌤️ Climate & Travel

The climate is tropical, with temperatures ranging from 24°C to 32°C. The dry season from March to May is ideal for travel, allowing tourists to fully enjoy beaches, mountain retreats, and eco-parks.
