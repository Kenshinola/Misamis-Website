Cagayan De Oro

Known as the "City of Golden Friendship," CDO is the regional center for commerce, education, and adventure in Northern Mindanao — and the birthplace of whitewater rafting in the Philippines.

🗺️ Geography & Location

Situated along the northern coast of Mindanao facing Macajalar Bay, CDO covers approximately 312 square kilometers. The city is surrounded by rivers, hills, and coastal areas — a strategic economic hub and convenient base for exploring mountains, waterfalls, and rivers nearby.

👥 Population

As of the 2020 census, Cagayan de Oro has an estimated population of 728,402 people. The city is home to residents from across the Philippines, creating a rich cultural mix reflected in its festivals, cuisine, and daily life.

🏗️ Economy

CDO thrives as a major commercial, industrial, and educational hub. Key industries include business process outsourcing, manufacturing, trade, and tourism. The Cagayan de Oro Port and the nearby Laguindingan Airport strengthen its role as a regional economic center.

Tourism & Attractions

CDO River

Thrilling whitewater rafting suitable for both beginners and experienced thrill-seekers.

Mapawa Nature Park

A scenic eco-destination featuring waterfalls, hiking trails, and outdoor activities.

Macahambus Cave & Gorge

A stunning natural gorge with historical significance and dramatic views.

City Museum

Showcases the history, culture, and heritage of Cagayan de Oro through engaging exhibits.

St. Augustine Cathedral

An iconic religious landmark known for its beautiful architecture and spiritual significance.

City Life, Parks & Markets

Vibrant parks and local markets offering a perfect mix of leisure, shopping, and culture.

🎉 Culture & Festivals

CDO celebrates the Higalaay Festival every August — a lively event featuring street parades, food fairs, and cultural performances. The city's residents are known for their friendliness and hospitality, earning it the nickname "City of Golden Friendship."

Education

Liceo De Cagayan University

One of the largest universities in the city, recognized for comprehensive programs and modern facilities.

Xavier University – Ateneo de Cagayan

A premier Jesuit institution known for academic excellence and strong community engagement.

Cagayan de Oro College – PHINMA

A student-centered institution providing affordable and industry-relevant courses.

🚌 Transportation

CDO is well-connected via Laguindingan Airport, linking it to Manila, Cebu, and other major cities. The city also has extensive road networks, buses, and jeepneys for easy travel within the city and to nearby tourist destinations.

🌤️ Climate

The city experiences a tropical climate with temperatures ranging from 24°C to 32°C. The dry season from March to May is ideal for outdoor activities and sightseeing.

✨ Fun Fact

CDO is considered the "Adventure Capital of Mindanao" and the birthplace of whitewater rafting in the Philippines. The city is also famous for pastel — a sweet filled bread — and fresh seafood dishes that make it a destination for food lovers too.
