Gingoog City

A coastal gem of Misamis Oriental known for stunning waterfalls, beautiful beaches, and a vibrant culture shaped by centuries of history and diversity.

📜 History & Culture

Gingoog was originally a mission settlement during the Spanish era and gradually developed through agriculture and trade. The influx of settlers from various regions created a rich cultural diversity. The majority speaks Cebuano, while indigenous languages such as Higaonon are still present due to historical settlement patterns.

🗺️ Geography & Location

Gingoog City spans approximately 568 square kilometers and is situated around Gingoog Bay, about 122 km east of Cagayan de Oro City and 74 km west of Butuan City. Composed of 79 barangays — from coastal communities to upland villages — it offers a diverse mix of landscapes from beaches to mountains.

🧭 Quick Facts

Gingoog City is a component city of Misamis Oriental, culturally diverse and reflecting influences from indigenous groups, Visayan settlers, and Mindanaoan traditions. Its tropical climate features warm, humid conditions with a distinct rainy season.

Natural Attractions & Tourism

Tiklas Falls

A stunning waterfall nestled in the mountains, perfect for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

Kilubag Falls

A serene waterfall surrounded by lush greenery, ideal for relaxation and nature appreciation.

Gantongon Falls

A hidden gem offering a tranquil escape with cascading waters and natural pools.

Burias Island

Pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant marine life — perfect for snorkeling and beach getaways.
